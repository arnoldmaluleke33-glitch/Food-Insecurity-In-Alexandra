Unemployment & Crime

High unemployment in Alexandra does not only lead to poverty, but can also increase crime rates. Many individuals, facing hunger and financial pressure, may turn to illegal activities as a way to survive and provide for their families. This creates a cycle where poverty leads to crime, and crime further destabilizes the community.

Solutions & Hope

Despite the challenges, there are real solutions that can help reduce food insecurity in Alexandra. These solutions focus on empowering communities, creating opportunities, and improving access to food.


Community feeding schemes and soup kitchens
Urban farming and local food gardens
Job creation and skills development

Campaign Planning

Timeline

The campaign runs from April to June. In April, the team prepares and plants a food garden with fast-growing crops like spinach and beetroot, while starting book drives and fixing sanitation issues. In May, progress is monitored, books are distributed, and infrastructure improvements continue. By June, crops are harvested to support the school feeding scheme and the overall impact is evaluated.


Resources Needed

The project requires consistent food supplies, funding, volunteers, cooking equipment, and transport. Support from local businesses and community members is essential to maintain the feeding programme and ensure sustainability.


Community Involvement

Teachers, learners, parents, and local businesses all play a role. Teachers identify learners in need, learners help raise awareness, and community members contribute food, time, and support to ensure the programme succeeds.


Skills Required

Key skills include organisation, teamwork, communication, leadership, and problem-solving. These ensure the campaign runs smoothly and adapts to challenges such as food shortages or logistical issues.
